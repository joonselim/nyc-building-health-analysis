What This Is

Daisy grows by winning buildings from underperforming management companies. This tool quantifies which buildings are most likely to be unhappy with their current management — using NYC's publicly available HPD violation data.

Every building in New York receives a Building Health Score (0–100): lower score = more unresolved violations, slower response, more hazardous issues. Low-scoring buildings are the ones most likely to consider switching management companies.

Note: This analysis does not identify which buildings Daisy currently manages — that data is internal. The scores here reflect management quality signals derived solely from public HPD records. A Daisy sales team could layer in their own portfolio to exclude existing clients and focus outreach on the rest.

Methodology

Score is a weighted composite of five components, each normalized 0–100 with min-max scaling across the full dataset. Higher component score = better management on that dimension.

30%
Open Violation Count
Total unresolved violations. More = lower score.
25%
Violation Severity
Class C × 3, Class B × 2, Class A × 1. Weighted sum.
20%
Resolution Speed
Avg days to close a violation. Slower = lower score.
15%
Recency
Violations issued in the last 6 months. More recent = lower score.
10%
Repeat Violation Rate
Same violation type appearing multiple times = lower score.
