The signal

Unresolved HPD violations are
a leading indicator of bad management.

Buildings with high volumes of open violations, slow resolution times, and repeat complaints are almost always buildings whose boards are already frustrated with their property manager — before they start looking for a replacement.

NYC HPD · Housing Maintenance Code Violations

Publicly updated daily by the NYC Department of Housing Preservation and Development. Every violation issued against a residential building carries a severity class, inspection date, and resolution status — government-issued, timestamped, and not self-reported.

Analyzed 19,170 buildings and 81,137 open violations. Of those, 28 matter for Daisy — and the rest of this deck is about who they are.

03
Building health score

A weighted composite, 0–100.

Each component is normalized across all buildings, then combined with explicit weights. Lower score = worse management = hotter prospect.

30%
Open violations
Total unresolved. More = lower score.
25%
Severity
Class C ×3, B ×2, A ×1. Weighted sum.
20%
Resolution speed
Avg days to close. Slower = lower score.
15%
Recency
Violations in last 6 months.
10%
Repeat rate
Same violation type recurring.
score = 0.30·open + 0.25·severity + 0.20·resolution + 0.15·recency + 0.10·repeat

Product planning

Audible Read & Listen — scroll-to-seek prototype

Live demo audiblereadandlisten…vercel.app/player ↗

Product thesis

Audiobook text should not be chained to the playhead. Readers want to browse ahead, jump back, and read-without-listening — the current UX doesn't let them.

Interaction design

  • Locked (default) — text auto-scrolls with audio
  • First scroll surfaces a "scroll up to unlock" hint
  • Unlocked — text freezes, every word becomes tappable
  • Tap a word → playback seeks to that word, text re-locks
  • "Go back to 1:49" pill remembers the pre-seek position

Precedents studied

YouTube Music · lyrics Spotify · lyrics Audible mobile app

Why

Music apps solved scroll-to-seek on lyrics years ago. Long-form reading deserves the same freedom — and the fix is interaction design, not a new model or backend.

SHIS — Shift Handover Intelligence System

Live demo · GitHub joonselim.github.io/sea_tech_summit… ↗

Transformation goal

Replace an unreliable, paper-based shift-handover ritual with a structured, auditable, 2-minute workflow — standardized across operators, supervisors, and plant managers.

Interventions

  • Structured capture UI in place of paper notebooks
  • Bridge the silo between machine alarms and operator tacit knowledge
  • Auto-generated standardized shift report
  • Digital sign-off — accountability across every shift

Stakeholders & scope

Plant operators Shift supervisors Plant manager Target: time-to-context < 2 min

Why

Manual handover is where production minutes disappear. Built as a POC for the Microsoft Atlanta Buildathon's AI-workforce-transformation track (won 2nd place) — the change is organizational, not just technological.

Development

Phone Agent — natural-language Android control

GitHub github.com/joonselim/phone-agent-try ↗

Intent

Control an Android phone entirely by typing natural-language commands — "order me KFC", "open Uber to my office".

Architecture

  • Claude parses user intent and extracts parameters
  • Router dispatches to service handlers (Uber Eats, Amazon)
  • Droidrun + ADB drive the device without any user taps

Stack

Claude API (Sonnet) Droidrun SDK ADB Python 3.12 uv

Why

Hands-free device control is where LLMs actually pay off — describe what you want, the agent operates the app. A US-adapted fork of DDalkkak (Korean original), rebuilt around US services.
